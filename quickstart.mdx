## Get up and running

This guide will take you from zero to your first workspace with pages, databases, and collaboration in just a few minutes.

<Note>
  **New to Brainbox?** Try the [online demo](https://brainbox.sh/) first, or follow this guide to install locally.
</Note>

## Step 1: Choose your platform

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web app">
    The fastest way to get started:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Visit brainbox.sh">
        Open [brainbox.sh](https://brainbox.sh/) in a modern browser (Chrome, Edge, or Firefox)
        
        <Warning>
          Safari and mobile browsers are not currently supported due to OPFS requirements.
        </Warning>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create an account">
        Click **Sign up** and create your account with email or Google
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify your email">
        Check your inbox and click the verification link (or use automatic verification if enabled)
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    For the best offline experience:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Download the installer">
        Get the latest release from [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/brainbox/brainbox/releases)
        
        - **macOS**: Download `.dmg` file
        - **Windows**: Download `.exe` installer  
        - **Linux**: Download `.deb` or `.rpm` package
      </Step>
      <Step title="Install Brainbox">
        Run the installer and follow the platform-specific instructions
      </Step>
      <Step title="Launch and sign in">
        Open Brainbox and create your account or sign in to an existing server
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Self-hosted">
    Deploy your own instance:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Prerequisites">
        Ensure you have Docker and Docker Compose installed
      </Step>
      <Step title="Clone and configure">
        ```bash
        git clone https://github.com/brainbox/brainbox.git
        cd brainbox/hosting/docker
        cp .env.example .env
        ```
        
        Edit `.env` and set secure passwords for PostgreSQL, Redis, and MinIO
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start services">
        ```bash
        docker compose up -d
        ```
        
        Access your instance at `http://localhost:4000`
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    
    <Tip>
      See the [Docker installation guide](/self-hosting/docker) for detailed configuration options.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Step 2: Create your first workspace

Workspaces are containers for all your content—pages, databases, files, and chat channels.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'New workspace'">
    After signing in, click the **+ New workspace** button in the sidebar
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your workspace">
    Give it a descriptive name like "Personal" or "Team Docs"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an icon (optional)">
    Select an emoji or upload a custom icon to personalize your workspace
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 3: Add your first page

Pages are the foundation of Brainbox—they support rich text, blocks, and nested content.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new page">
    In your workspace, click **+ New page** or press `Cmd/Ctrl + N`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a title">
    Click the title area and type your page name
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start writing">
    Click in the editor and start typing. Try these commands:
    
    - Type `/` to see the block menu
    - `# Heading` for headings
    - `- List item` for bullet lists  
    - `[ ] Task` for checkboxes
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add nested pages">
    Hover over a page in the sidebar and click **+** to create a sub-page
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Pages auto-save as you type—no need to manually save!
</Tip>

## Step 4: Create a database

Databases let you organize structured data with custom fields and multiple views.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a database">
    Click **+ New** → **Database** or type `/database` in a page
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your database">
    Give it a name like "Tasks" or "Projects"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add custom fields">
    Click **+ Add field** to create columns:
    
    - **Text**: Names, descriptions, URLs
    - **Number**: Quantities, prices, scores
    - **Select**: Categories, statuses, priorities  
    - **Date**: Deadlines, due dates
    - **Checkbox**: Completed, active flags
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add records">
    Click **+ New record** to add rows to your database
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch views">
    Click the view dropdown to toggle between:
    
    - **Table**: Spreadsheet-style grid
    - **Kanban**: Card-based board grouped by select field
    - **Calendar**: Timeline view for date fields
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 5: Collaborate with your team

Invite others to collaborate on your workspace with granular permissions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Invite members">
    Click **Settings** → **Members** → **Invite**
    
    Enter their email address and choose a role:
    - **Admin**: Full access including settings
    - **Member**: Can create and edit content  
    - **Guest**: View-only or limited access
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share specific pages">
    Right-click any page → **Share** → Add specific collaborators
  </Step>
  <Step title="Real-time editing">
    When multiple users edit the same page, you'll see their cursors and changes in real-time
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 6: Try chat and files

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Chat">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create a channel">
        Click **+ New** → **Channel** to create a team chat
      </Step>
      <Step title="Send messages">
        Type and press Enter to send. Use `@mention` to notify teammates
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add collaborators">
        Click channel settings → **Add members** to invite specific people
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Files">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Upload files">
        Drag and drop files into any page or click **+ New** → **File**
      </Step>
      <Step title="Organize in folders">
        Create folders to keep files organized by project or category
      </Step>
      <Step title="Preview and download">
        Click any file to preview or download it
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
